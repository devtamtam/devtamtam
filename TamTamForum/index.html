Since May of last year, I have been studying fluid numerical analysis in the Honda Laboratory. The goal of this research is to find a good way to cool a heated flat plate using a jet of air using numerical simulations.

13:20~13:30 break
13:30~13:55 Research Presentation Fumihiro Ohashi Kubo Lab (Spectroscopy) B3 Development of a novel Structural Analysis Method for proteins using infrared spectroscopy and computational science
Detail

Analysing structural dynamics of protein is now siginificant to figure out not only the structure but how proteins work, especially when they are intrinsically disordered. However, the conventional ways (e.x. NMR, Xray crystallography ,etc.) have limited performance. Spectroscopy and computational science also have the negative point, but they can Compensate for each other's weaknesses. So, we are searching the novel way. In today's talk, you'll know the potentiality of Infrared spectroscopy and computational science.
